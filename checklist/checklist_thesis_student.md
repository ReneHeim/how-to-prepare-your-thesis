# Thesis Checklist (B.Sc. & M.Sc.)

This checklist summarises what is expected in Bachelor and Master theses.  
Master theses are expected to show greater depth, independence, and originality.

---

## 1. Topic, Aim & Research Questions

- [ ] Topic is clearly delimited (what, where, when) and relevant.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Clear, focused topic in a well-defined study area.  
- Relevance is explained mainly based on existing literature and practical examples.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Topic positioned more explicitly within a research debate.  
- Relevance framed with respect to current theoretical and/or methodological discussions.

---

## 2. State of the Art / Literature

- [ ] Literature review leads logically to the research gap and questions.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Solid overview of key studies and concepts directly related to the topic.  
- Correct use of concepts; limited but coherent critical reflection.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Broader and more systematic coverage of the relevant literature (international journals, methods papers).  
- Clear identification of gaps, controversies, or open questions; explicit critical discussion.

---

## 3. Data & Methods (Concept)

- [ ] Data and methods are appropriate and transparently planned.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Use of established datasets and standard methods taught in courses (e.g. basic spatial analysis, descriptive statistics).  
- Clear description of preprocessing and methods, mainly following best practice from teaching and literature.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Possible integration of more complex or multiple data sources; own data collection and/or advanced processing steps.  
- Methodological choices argued more independently (why this method, alternatives, limitations).

---

## 4. Implementation with GIS & R

- [ ] GIS/R implementation is structured and reproducible.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Clean project and folder structure (`data/`, `R/`, `output/`, etc.).  
- Well-organised scripts that reproduce the main analysis; basic commenting and documentation.

**M.Sc.:**  
- More sophisticated workflows (e.g. modular R scripts, functions, automation, reproducible pipelines).  
- Strong emphasis on reproducibility (e.g. clear session info, package versions, script to run full analysis end-to-end) and robust error handling.

---

## 5. Results

- [ ] Results clearly answer the research questions.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Results structured by research questions; main patterns clearly described.  
- Figures/maps/tables correctly produced and explained.

**M.Sc.:**  
- More nuanced and detailed analysis (e.g. model diagnostics, sensitivity tests, multiple scenarios).  
- Results critically evaluated, including strengths/weaknesses of the evidence.

---

## 6. Discussion

- [ ] Results are interpreted and discussed in a broader context.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Interpretation relates results to the initial aims and to selected literature.  
- Some reflection on uncertainties and limitations.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Deeper critical engagement with theory and empirical findings from the literature.  
- Systematic discussion of uncertainties, limitations, and implications for future research and/or practice.

---

## 7. Conclusion & Outlook

- [ ] Main findings and implications are clearly summarised.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Concise summary; explicit answers to research questions.  
- Short outlook with realistic suggestions for future work.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Clear statement of the thesis’ contribution (theoretical, methodological, or applied).  
- More elaborated outlook, possibly including concrete research designs, methodological improvements, or policy/management implications.

---

## 8. Sources & Referencing

- [ ] Scientific sources and correct citation practice.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Use of relevant textbooks and journal articles; correct and consistent referencing.  
- Citation of data sources, software, and R packages.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Strong focus on peer-reviewed, international literature and method papers.  
- More systematic referencing (e.g. covering key schools of thought, competing methods).
---

## 9. Form, Language & Layout

- [ ] Formal and linguistic quality supports scientific communication.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Mostly correct spelling and grammar, generally clear style.  
- Proper structure, figures, tables, and maps with complete legends and metadata.

**M.Sc.:**  
- High standard in academic writing (precision, coherence, appropriate register).  
- Very good layout and graphical communication; professionally prepared figures/maps.

---

## 10. Independence & Project Management

- [ ] Work demonstrates appropriate independence and time management.

**B.Sc.:**  
- Able to work independently within a clearly defined project; regular guidance from the supervisor.  
- Keeps deadlines and responds to feedback.

**M.Sc.:**  
- Designs and steers a more complex project with higher autonomy (problem definition, design decisions, troubleshooting).  
- Proactive planning, regular critical reflection, and efficient use of supervision.
