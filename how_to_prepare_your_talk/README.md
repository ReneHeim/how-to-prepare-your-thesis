# Master Thesis Examination/Colloquium Preparation Guide

## 1. Structure Your Presentation

- **Introduction:** Briefly introduce yourself and the context of your research.
- **Motivation & Problem Statement:** Clearly articulate the research question or problem you addressed.
- **Objectives:** State the goals or hypotheses of your thesis.
- **Methods/Approach:** Summarize your research methodology or analytical framework.
- **Results:** Present your main findings with concise visuals (graphs, tables).
- **Discussion/Interpretation:** Interpret the implications of your findings; acknowledge limitations.
- **Conclusion & Future Work:** Provide take-home messages and suggestions for future research.

## 2. Design Effective Slides

- Use readable fonts and limit text per slide.
- Employ visuals (charts, diagrams) to illustrate complex data.
- Avoid clutter—each slide should convey one clear idea.
- Practice your timing; aim for a 10-20 minute presentation unless instructed otherwise.

## 3. Rehearse Actively

- Practice aloud with friends, colleagues, or mentors—especially with those unfamiliar with your work.
- Simulate the Q&A by having them ask challenging or unexpected questions.
- Record yourself to check pacing and clarity.

## 4. Prepare for Questions

- Review your entire thesis—know your methods, data, and literature inside out.
- Prepare answers to common types of questions:
  - *Clarifying questions:* Be ready to explain key concepts or methods simply.
  - *Critical questions:* Defend your choices, but acknowledge limitations when appropriate.
  - *“What if?” or future direction questions:* Show openness and awareness of broader implications.
- If you don’t know the answer, it’s OK to admit it or suggest how you’d investigate further.

## 5. Presentation Day Tips

- Arrive 30 min early, test your equipment, and set up your slides.
- Bring a bottle of water.
- Start with a confident introduction and make eye contact with your audience.
- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace.
- Use pointer or gestures to emphasize key points, but don’t overdo it.
- For audience questions, listen carefully, repeat the question if necessary, and answer directly.
- Thank the audience at the conclusion.

## 6. Professionalism and Mindset

- View questions as an opportunity to showcase your expertise and to explore new ideas, not as an interrogation.
- Stay calm—nerves are normal and show you care.

## 7. After the Colloquium

- Reflect on feedback, both positive and critical.
- Consider updating your thesis or future research directions based on suggestions received.


*Good luck on your thesis examination!*
